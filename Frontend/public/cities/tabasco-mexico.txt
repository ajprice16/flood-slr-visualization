Tabasco, Mexico faces recurrent flooding due to its low-lying coastal plains, major river systems (Grijalva–Usumacinta), and intense rainfall.
 Sea level rise exacerbates river backflow and storm surge, increasing risk for communities around Villahermosa and the coastal wetlands. 
 Infrastructure and wetlands restoration, drainage improvements, and resilient urban planning are key adaptation strategies.
